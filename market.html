Planning an NFT project

Step 1: Know your Target Audience

The first step before starting any project should be to know who your targeted audience is. Before defining the concept or story of your NFT project, decide what kind of community you will target. For example, if you are creating NFT games, you should design this project related explicitly to teenagers and youngsters as people from this age group are most interested in games. Remember that a strong community makes an NFT project successful and popular, so prepare about the demands your targeted community will ask for. You should keep the following points in mind while deciding your target audience or community:

  • What is my target audience preferring nowadays?
  • Are there any new trends that this particular community is looking for?
  • Always prioritize the demands and requirements of your targeted community.
  • Make a roadmap always to engage and grab the attention of your targeted audience through different interesting activities.

Step 2: Define the Concept of your NFT Project

It is undoubtedly the most significant step of the whole planning phase of your NFT project. NFT marketplaces offer a wide range of digital assets like artworks, painting, games, popular tweets, memes, stickers, and the list goes on. Every NFT project in the market has a certain concept and meaning behind it, such as digital artists creating unique artworks and then auctioning them on NFT marketplaces, and ultimately they earn incentives through it. Similarly, in NFT games, users are asked to buy tokens to increase their winning chances. So firstly, plan out the main concept of your NFT project by exploring different NFT projects from the internet and looking at their specifications. You should ask yourself the below questions while deciding the concept of your project:

  • Who is my target audience?
  • Is my NFT project offering this audience something new and unique?
  • What is the scope of the project?
  • Is my NFT project viable in the long run?

Step 3: Explore other NFT Collections

If you are a beginner or new to the NFT world, it’s better to get aware of the market trends by researching other NFT collections that are popular and creative. One amazing thing about NFT collections is that you are not restricted to limited options, as you can paint your organic ideas on a blank canvass. Remember that every NFT item needs to be unique and scarce as it cannot be replicated. So to get some inspiration and major ideas to build your own NFT collections, you can explore other famous NFT collections by searching them. The famous NFT collections are:

  • CryptoPunks: An infamous NFT project that includes more than 10,000 art pieces.
  • The Bored Ape Yacht Club: A famous collection of 10000 unique Bored Ape NFTs
  • Azuki: A collection of 10,000 avatars that give you membership access
  • World of Women: A community celebrating representation, inclusivity, and equal opportunities for all.

The examples mentioned above are just some of the notable NFT collections that one should explore before planning their NFT project. You can search other NFT collections to get some more ideas about how NFT collections should look.

Step 4: Choosing an Art Style

After deciding on your concept and exploring different NFT collections, it’s time to choose your art style. Your art style should match your targeted audience as their preferences directly impact the success of an NFT project. The art style of your NFT project can be anything like a song or a video game. You can get inspiration from tons of NFT collections and artworks sold at high prices. Choose the category of your art styles such as illustrations, 3D paintings, tickets, or pixel arts. Choosing the category will simplify the process of deciding your art style further.

As the peculiarity of your NFT collection defines the authenticity and ownership of your NFT project, make sure that you don’t replicate other’s art styles, as every NFT item needs to be unique. While deciding your art style, check whether it’s visually appealing or not. Also, evaluate what kind of innovation you are bringing in your art style to make your NFT project stand out among other projects.

Step 5: Choosing a Project Name

As you know that the first impression is the last. So, choosing an attractive yet well-defined project name would prove to be your first impression on the audience. A project name needs to be concise yet well-defined so that the audience would get some idea at first glance about your NFT project. This step holds special importance as it will work as a clickbait for the audience. Your project name must be distinctive, which should hold some curiosity for your audience. So do your research and build a project name that collaborates with your art style and targeted audience.

Step 6: Defining Project Details

This step all the technicalities that an NFT project requires, such as pricing details, timelines, and distribution of your NFT items. To determine the price of an NFT collection, check and evaluate the market and real-world values and factors. The value of NFT items also depends on marketplaces such as OpenSea and Valuable. By assessing all the market and real-world forces, you would develop a suitable price for NFT assets.

Furthermore, your roadmap should also include the timeline of activities that will work as a catalyst to boost the popularity of your project. Make a practical timeline about all the countdowns, hashtags, and giveaways that will help to increase the engagement of your community. Draw a chart and plan your activities according to the decided timeline to avoid panic at the last minute.

Conclusion

It’s not easy to create an NFT project from scratch without having prior experience in the NFT world. But planning and creating a practical roadmap will ease things for you in the future. This article will work as an effective beginner guide for individuals who want to create their NFT projects without any professional help.
